# 🛡️ Stay Safe Online: A Checklist for Indian College Students (2026)

Digital safety is no longer just about choosing a strong password; it's about being vigilant against sophisticated AI scams and protecting your financial identity. Use this checklist to stay secure.

---

### 📑 The Digital Safety Checklist

1.  **Never Scan a QR Code to "Receive" Money:** This is the most common UPI scam. Remember: Scanning a QR code is *only* for making a payment. If someone asks you to scan a code to receive a prize or payment, it is a scam. 💸
2.  **Guard your UPI PIN & OTPs Like Cash:** No bank official, customer care executive, or "buyer" will ever ask for your UPI PIN or a One-Time Password (OTP). If they do, hang up immediately.
3.  **Use Hardware-Based MFA:** In 2026, SMS-based authentication can be bypassed. Whenever possible, use an Authenticator App (like Google Authenticator) or biometric (Fingerprint/Face ID) locks for your sensitive apps.
4.  **Verify Before You Click (The "Long-Press" Rule):** Before clicking any link in a WhatsApp message or email, long-press it to see the actual URL. Scammers often use "look-alike" domains (e.g., `amozon.in` instead of `amazon.in`).
5.  **Audit App Permissions Regularly:** Go to your phone settings and check which apps have access to your "Microphone," "Camera," and "Contacts." Delete any apps you haven't used in the last 3 months.
6.  **Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Sensitive Tasks:** Never log into your student portal, bank account, or UPI app using free public Wi-Fi (like at cafes or railway stations) unless you are using a trusted VPN.
7.  **Be Skeptical of "Urgent" Messages:** Scammers use AI to create a sense of panic (e.g., "Your electricity will be cut in 1 hour" or "Your SIM card is blocked"). Take a breath and verify these claims through official apps or websites only.
8.  **Beware of AI Deepfakes:** If a friend or family member calls you from an unknown number asking for money using a voice that sounds like theirs, ask them a personal question only they would know. AI can now mimic voices perfectly.

---

### 🚨 How to Report Cybercrime
If you or someone you know falls victim to a scam, **speed is critical.** Reporting within the first "Golden Hour" significantly increases the chances of freezing the stolen funds.

* **Official Portal:** [www.cybercrime.gov.in](https://www.cybercrime.gov.in)
* **National Helpline:** Call **1930** immediately (Available 24/7).
* **Bank Action:** Immediately call your bank's dedicated fraud helpline to block your accounts and cards.
