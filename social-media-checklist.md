# 📱 Social Media Etiquette for College Students: Do's and Don'ts

Maintaining a professional and safe digital presence is essential for career growth and personal security. Use this checklist as a guide for responsible social media use.

---

### ✅ The "Do's" (Best Practices)
* **Do Audit Privacy Settings:** Regularly check your account settings every semester to ensure "Public" isn't your default for personal posts.
* **Do Use LinkedIn Professionally:** Showcase your campus projects, certifications, and volunteer work to build a "digital resume" for recruiters.
* **Do Follow the "Grandma Rule":** Before posting, ask yourself: *"Would I be comfortable if my grandmother or a future CEO saw this?"*
* **Do Fact-Check Before Sharing:** Verify news or viral "hacks" using reputable sources before hitting share to avoid spreading misinformation.
* **Do Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Protect your accounts from being hijacked by adding an extra layer of security beyond just a password.

---

### ❌ The "Don'ts" (Common Pitfalls)
* **Don't Post Sensitive Info:** Never share your real-time location, dorm room number, student ID, or photos of your house keys/mail.
* **Don't Vent About Work or School:** Avoid complaining about professors, internships, or coworkers. These "rants" often find their way back to the people involved.
* **Don't Post "Red Cup" Photos:** Avoid sharing content that depicts illegal activity or violations of university policy (like underage drinking or drug use).
* **Don't Engage with Trolls:** Avoid participating in online arguments or cyberbullying. Even a "like" on a mean comment is a digital footprint that links back to you.
* **Don't Recycle Passwords:** Never use the same password for all your social platforms. If one app is breached, all your accounts are at risk.
