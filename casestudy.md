# Case Study: UPI and Online Payment Fraud

**UPI (Unified Payments Interface) Fraud** is a deceptive practice where scammers trick individuals into authorizing unauthorized transactions from their bank accounts using mobile payment apps. In an increasingly digital economy, this crime has become rampant because it exploits the speed of transactions and a common misunderstanding of how digital payment protocols work.

### How the Scam Happens (Step-by-Step)
1.  **The Bait:** A scammer contacts a victim through an online marketplace or social media, pretending to be a buyer interested in an item the victim is selling.
2.  **The Deception:** The scammer claims they want to pay the full amount immediately but insists that "technical issues" require the victim to scan a "Payment QR Code" to *receive* the funds.
3.  **The Pressure:** The scammer sends a QR code via a messaging app. They use social engineering—acting in a hurry or being overly friendly—to distract the victim from reading the app's on-screen prompts carefully.
4.  **The Theft:** When the victim scans the code, the payment app asks for their **UPI PIN**. Since a PIN is only required to *send* money, entering it instantly authorizes a transfer *out* of the victim’s account to the scammer.

### Target Audience & Consequences
Scammers typically target students, small business owners, and older adults who may not be fully aware of the golden rule: **receiving money never requires a PIN.** The consequences are both financial and psychological. Victims often suffer immediate, non-reversible loss of savings. Beyond the monetary hit, it leads to significant emotional distress and a long-term loss of trust in digital financial tools.
